"Ride, Trick, Repeat – Mastering the Art of Skateboarding"

A quick glance at the topics you'll learn

A skateboard is a flat board with four wheels that people ride and perform tricks on. It’s not just a mode of transportation — it’s a lifestyle and a popular extreme sport practiced by millions around the world.

Deck: The wooden board where the rider stands. Usually made from layers of maple wood.
Trucks: Metal T-shaped parts that connect the wheels to the deck and allow turning.
Wheels: Usually made of polyurethane, they vary in size and hardness based on the rider’s style.
Bearings: Small metal rings that go inside the wheels to help them spin smoothly.
Grip Tape: A sandpaper-like sheet stuck to the top of the deck for traction.

Street: Performed in urban areas using stairs, rails, benches, etc.
Vert: Involves ramps and halfpipes, focusing on air tricks.
Cruising: Smooth riding, often for transportation or casual fun.
Freestyle: Technical tricks performed on flat ground.

1. Get the Right Gear: Wear a helmet, knee pads, elbow pads, and skate shoes.

2. Find Your Stance: Regular (left foot forward) or Goofy (right foot forward).

3. Balance: Practice standing on your board on grass or carpet to get used to it.

4. Push Off: Place your front foot on the board and push with your back foot, then place it on the board.

5. Turn: Lean your body slightly in the direction you want to turn.

6. Stop: Use your back foot to gently drag on the ground or press the tail down to slow down.

7. Practice: Find a smooth flat area like a driveway or park and ride often!
